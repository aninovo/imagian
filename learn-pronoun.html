PRONOUNS



Me, we, you alone, you all.

A silhouette pointing at itself with a thumb is ME. Add another one that's WE.

A silhouette pointed at with an index finger is YOU. Add another one and it's plural YOU, like Y'ALL.



This, that.

Point at the place where you are: this means THIS. THIS is what's closer to the speaker.

The opposite is THAT: something (or someone) over there.

Like in some other languages, THAT can also mean "he", "she" or "it" depending on the context.



What?

The following symbols belong to those few taken from math: "there exist" for SOME, "for each" for EACH and EVERY and an "empty set" for NO.



Some, each, no.

The planet in a square means ALL. Unlike EVERY, ALL means "all together" not "each individually".

The asterisk means ANY, just because.



Any, all.



Who?

It can be combined with the determiners THIS, THAT, ANY, SOME, EACH, ALL and NO to form pronoun referring to people.



This one, that one (he / she), anybody.



Nobody, somebody, everybody.

Combined with ALL it means "all" or "everyone", i.e. the people together. Combined with EVERY it stands for "each one", i.e. each separate individual.



Each against all!
